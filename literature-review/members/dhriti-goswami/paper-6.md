# 📄 Paper 6: Advancing Genome Editing with Artificial Intelligence: Opportunities, Challenges, and Future Directions
*   **Journal / Conference**: Frontiers in Bioengineering and Biotechnology (2023)
*   **DOI**: `10.3389/fbioe.2023.1256871`

---

## 🔬 Literature Review Table

| Evaluation Field | Researcher Analysis |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Problem Addressed** <br>*(1-2 lines)* | The paper explores the contribution of AI(designing guide RNAs or using tools like DeepCRISPR, DeepHF) in improving CRISPR based genome editing technology. |
| **Methodology & Approach** <br>*(2-3 lines)* | The review synthesizes literature via PRISMA methodology across PubMed, Scopus, Medline, and Google Scholar, categorizing ML/DL tools (CNNs, RNNs, SVMs, attention-based models) by application area - gRNA design, base editing, prime editing, and epigenome editing and evaluating their performance against on-target/off-target prediction benchmarks. |
| **Key Results & Findings** <br>*(2-3 lines)* | AI optimises GED by selecting appropriate technique (BED,PED,epi-GED) for the target sequence and improves delivery through better designed vectors, promoters and enhancers. It also enables personalised treatment options as well as monitoring ongoing treatment response. |
| **Limitations Identified** <br>*(1-2 lines)* | There are several challenges to this tech which includes high costs, potential off-target effects, efficiency of on-target editing. Incorporating AI models in healthcare, it is imperative to prioritise patient privacy, being ethically considerate and compliance with regulations. |
| **Future Scope** <br>*(2-3 lines)* | Future work should focus on areas such as transfer learning, network design optimisation, explainability, informative feature selection, uncertainty quantification and expanding beyond on/off -target predictions. |
| **Project Relevance** <br>*(1-2 lines)* | Methodologically relevant - the CNN/attention-based models used for gRNA and off-target prediction parallel the deep learning approaches in our Whole Genome Sequencing and Variant Prioritization pillars.|
