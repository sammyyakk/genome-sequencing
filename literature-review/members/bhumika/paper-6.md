# 📄 Paper 6: Whole-Genome Sequencing Surveillance and Machine Learning for Healthcare Outbreak Detection and Investigation: A Systematic Review and Summary
*   **Journal / Conference**: Antimicrobial Stewardship & Healthcare Epidemiology (2022)
*   **DOI**: `10.1017/ash.2021.241`

---

## 🔬 Literature Review Table

| Evaluation Field | Researcher Analysis |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Problem Addressed** <br>*(1-2 lines)* | _[Hospital outbreaks often go undetected using normal methods,this review checks if sequencing bacteria DNA + machine learning can catch outbreaks earlier and find how they actually spread.]_ |
| **Methodology & Approach** <br>*(2-3 lines)* | _[Systematic review of 42 studies that used WGS for outbreak detection and 4 studies that used machine learning/AI to trace transmission routes, searched via PubMed.]_ |
| **Key Results & Findings** <br>*(2-3 lines)* | _[97.6% of studies found genetically related (same outbreak) bacteria; 525 outbreaks were found in total. Most studies only looked at same ward, same time to guess how infection spread and very few used machine learning, even though it helped find hidden transmission routes (like shared equipment between wards)]_ |
| **Limitations Identified** <br>*(1-2 lines)* | _[Most studies were done after the outbreak has occured (retrospective), not in real-time; most looked at only 1 type of bacteria at a time; relying only on ward/time clustering ignoring real transmission routes.]_ |
| **Future Scope** <br>*(2-3 lines)* | _[Combining real-time WGS + electronic health records + machine learning could catch outbreaks faster and stop them before they spread further, improving patient safety and reducing costs.]_ |
| **Project Relevance** <br>*(1-2 lines)* | _[Shows how genome sequencing isn't just for individual diagnosis, it can also track disease spread across a population which broadens the real-world impact angle of our project.]_ |
