| **Problem Addressed** <br>*(1-2 lines)* | _[The paper focuses on underperformance of predictive models in real-world scenarios and the limited ability to interpret complex models to uncover meaningful biological insights.]_ |

| **Methodology & Approach** <br>*(2-3 lines)* | _[The researchers performed a analysis of deep learning approaches applied to genomics, classifying them into categories such as CNNs, RNNs,autoencoders, transformers, and hybrid models. These models were accessed in areas like gene expression analysis , regular genomics, protein structure and variant effect prediction.]_ |

| **Key Results & Findings** <br>*(2-3 lines)* | _[AlphaFold and AlphaFold2 used Transformer based neural networks with an attention mechanism to predict a protein's 3D shape from its amino acid sequence with very high accuracy. AlphaFold2 became famous as it performed the best in the CASP14 protein prediction challenge. Other models like DeepEnhancer, BiRen, DanQ, and ESM-2 help predict how DNA changes affect the genes and understanding protein structures. ]_ |

| **Limitations Identified** <br>*(1-2 lines)* | _[Despite the high accuracy , deep learning models lack the transparency and interpretability because the internal connections within the networks are complex and information spreads widely throughout the model. AlphaFold2 performs poor with those proteins which doesn't have a fixed 3D shape. LLM's also require high computational power making it expensive to use.]_ |

| **Future Scope** <br>*(2-3 lines)* | _[With the improving interpretability through generalized visualisation techniques ,attention scoring and importance measures applicable across deep learning models in genomics. The paper also expects genomic language models to keep scaling toward longer context windows and lower training costs.]_ |
