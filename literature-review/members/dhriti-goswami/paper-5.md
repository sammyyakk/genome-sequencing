# 📄 Paper 5: Artificial Intelligence in Clinical and Genomic Diagnostics
*   **Journal / Conference**: Genome Medicine (2018)
*   **DOI**: `10.1186/s13073-018-0589-9`

---

## 🔬 Literature Review Table

| Evaluation Field | Researcher Analysis |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Problem Addressed** <br>*(1-2 lines)* | The paper explores the application of emerging methods (deep learning and NLP) for specific tasks in clinical genomics like variant calling, genomic annotation, variant classification, phenotype to genotype mapping. |
| **Methodology & Approach** <br>*(2-3 lines)* | AI methods have been categorised into 5 domains – computer vision, time series analysis, speech recognition, NLP and explains how deep learning architecture like CNN and RNN learn hierarchical features directly from raw data like EHR codes, DNA Sequencing. |
| **Key Results & Findings** <br>*(2-3 lines)* | Deep learning has achieved strong performance in genomics and healthcare tasks such as variant calling, genome annotation, disease-risk prediction, and phenotype analysis. Advanced models like DeepVariant, DANN, PrimateAI, DeepSEA, and DeepGestalt consistently outperform traditional methods, while NLP and speech-based AI can accurately predict clinical outcomes and detect diseases from health records and vocal biomarkers. |
| **Limitations Identified** <br>*(1-2 lines)* |AI is dependent on large scale datasets for training and scalable collection of phenotype data is difficult as this type of collection requires numerous data and is highly variable across collection site.   |
| **Future Scope** <br>*(2-3 lines)* | Future work should focus on developing generalizable AI models using diverse global datasets (like UK Biobank, All of Us) and multimodal health data to improve disease prediction and personalized treatment. It should also focus on reducing bias, increasing interpretability, enabling continuous model updates. |
