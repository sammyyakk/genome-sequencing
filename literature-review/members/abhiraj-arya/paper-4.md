| **Problem Addressed** <br>*(1-2 lines)* | _[Spatial transtranscriptomics methods can show where genes are active in a tissue, but each spot often contains RNA from multiple cells instead of single cell. This mixing makes creates a challenge in identifying individual cell types and their location.]_ |


| **Methodology & Approach** <br>*(2-3 lines)* | _[Cell2location , a statistical model that uses single cell RNA sequencing(scRNA) data. The model uses this data to estimate how much of RNA in each spatial spots come from different cell types.]_ |


| **Key Results & Findings** <br>*(2-3 lines)* | _[The model performed better than existing AI tools in testing with artificial data and real tissues. It identified the specefic subtypes of astrocytes in mouse thalamus . It also found a rare type of B-cell in the human lymph node.]_ |


| **Limitations Identified** <br>*(1-2 lines)* | _[It depends on the high quality scRNA-seq data. It is more of resource intensive than other models and also require a high GPU for training large datasets.]_ |


| **Future Scope** <br>*(2-3 lines)* | _[The results from this paper can be used to analyze the tissue structure , studying the cell and its interaction. It can also be applied to study more about the complex diseases. ]_ |
