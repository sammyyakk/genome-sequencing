# 📄 Paper 8: Machine Learning Enables Detection of Early-Stage Colorectal Cancer by Whole-Genome Sequencing of Plasma Cell-Free DNA
*   **Journal / Conference**: BMC Cancer (2019)
*   **DOI**: `10.1186/s12885-019-6003-8`

---

## 🔬 Literature Review Table

| Evaluation Field | Researcher Analysis |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Problem Addressed** <br>*(1-2 lines)* | _[Cancer screening methods are invasive and inconvenient. Current blood tests struggle to detect early-stage cancer since tumor DNA is very scarce in blood at that stage.]_ |
| **Methodology & Approach** <br>*(2-3 lines)* | _[Sequenced cell-free DNA from blood of 546 colorectal cancer patients and 271 healthy people; trained machine learning models on DNA patterns, tested the models carefully using several methods to rule out "cheating" via unrelated factors like age or lab batch.]_ |
| **Key Results & Findings** <br>*(2-3 lines)* | _[Achieved high accuracy (92% AUC) detecting cancer, including 80% early-stage cases, with 85% sensitivity at 85% specificity. Performance dropped a bit (71-85%) when controlling for confounding factors like institution bias, showing the importance of careful testing.]_ |
| **Limitations Identified** <br>*(1-2 lines)* | _[Data was collected retrospectively with potential sample-handling issues; couldn't fully separate which DNA signals came from the tumor vs. other body changes; results need confirmation in a separate independent study.]_ |
| **Future Scope** <br>*(2-3 lines)* | _[Plans for prospective (real-time, forward-looking) validation studies and combining DNA data with other markers (like proteins) for even better accuracy could extend to other cancer types too.]_ |
| **Project Relevance** <br>*(1-2 lines)* | _[Shows a powerful clinical application of genome sequencing (cancer screening from blood) and highlights the importance of properly validating machine learning models — useful if our project touches on diagnostic applications.]_ |
