# 📄 Paper 3: An integrated encyclopedia of DNA elements in the human genome
*   **Journal / Conference**: Nature (2012)
*   **DOI**: `10.1038/nature11247`
*   **Link**: [Nature](https://www.nature.com/articles/nature11247)

---

## 🔬 Literature Review Table

| Evaluation Field | Researcher Analysis |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Problem Addressed** <br>*(1-2 lines)* |  Despite the human genome being sequenced, the function of the vast majority of its nearly 3 billion bases remained unknown - protein-coding genes account for only ~2% of the genome, leaving the role of the remaining 98% largely uncharacterized. |
| **Methodology & Approach** <br>*(2-3 lines)* | The ENCODE (Encyclopedia of DNA Elements) Consortium generated 1,640 datasets across 147 cell types using a comprehensive suite of assays - RNA-seq, ChIP-seq, DNase-seq, FAIRE-seq, RRBS methylation sequencing, and chromosome conformation capture - to systematically map transcription, transcription factor binding, chromatin structure, histone modifications, and DNA methylation across the entire human genome. |
| **Key Results & Findings** <br>*(2-3 lines)* |  80.4% of the human genome was assigned at least one biochemical function, with 399,124 enhancer-like regions and 70,292 promoter-like regions identified. GWAS disease-associated SNPs were found to be significantly enriched in non-coding ENCODE-annotated functional regions (34% of GWAS SNPs overlap DNase hypersensitive sites), providing a major resource for interpreting non-coding genetic variation linked to disease. |
| **Limitations Identified** <br>*(1-2 lines)* |  The 80% functional figure is based on biochemical activity, not evolutionary conservation or proven biological necessity - many elements may be biochemically detectable but functionally neutral. Additionally, only a limited number of cell types were profiled, meaning the true proportion of functional elements across all human cell types is likely underestimated. |
| **Future Scope** <br>*(2-3 lines)* | The authors emphasize that ENCODE data should guide interpretation of GWAS results and personal genome variants, and that expanding the project to more cell types, developmental stages, and tissues will continue to reveal regulatory elements underlying human disease. |
| **Project Relevance** <br>*(1-2 lines)* | ENCODE directly underpins genome sequencing research by providing the functional annotation layer needed to interpret sequenced variants - without knowing which non-coding regions are regulatory, sequencing alone cannot explain why most disease-associated variants have the effects they do. |
