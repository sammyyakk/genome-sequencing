# 📄 Paper 7: Long-Read Human Genome Sequencing and its Applications
*   **Journal / Conference**: Nature Reviews Genetics (2020)
*   **DOI**: `10.1038/s41576-020-0236-x`

---

## 🔬 Literature Review Table

| Evaluation Field | Researcher Analysis |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Problem Addressed** <br>*(1-2 lines)* | The paper explores Long Read Sequencing technologies like PacBio(SMRT) and Oxford Nanopore Technology(ONT) against the short read one and then compared ONT and SMRT in terms of read accuracy, throughput, cost to short read technology along with practical applications of these technologies in genomics transcriptomics and epigenomics. |
| **Methodology & Approach** <br>*(2-3 lines)* | The paper describes the underlying chemistry of each tech (PacBio's zero-mode waveguide/circular consensus sequencing and ONT's nanopore electrical current detection), then surveys published applications across three areas: structural variant detection, genome assembly (including early telomere-to-telomere efforts), and epigenetic/transcriptomic profiling using long reads. |
| **Key Results & Findings** <br>*(2-3 lines)* | PacBio HiFi reads (10-20kb) achieve accuracy (>99%) compared to short-read levels through consensus sequencing while ONT offers much longer reads but with lower per-read accuracy. These technologies enabled some of the first telomere-to-telomere chromosome assemblies and direct detection of base modifications. |
| **Limitations Identified** <br>*(1-2 lines)* | Limitations include the underlying differences in the chemistry of ONT and SMRT tech along with sequence detection approaches influence the read length, base accuracy. High-cost factor is a major hurdle in its application. |
| **Future Scope** <br>*(2-3 lines)* | Future work should focus on new emerging tech like light sampling for variant discovery and development of Graph based reference genome using Variant Graph toolkit. Functional data will be superimposed on genomes including epigenetic and transcriptomic differences that occur at cellular and developmental level. |
| **Project Relevance** <br>*(1-2 lines)* | The paper is relevant to our variant prioritisation pillar of our project as it deals with ONT and SMRT tech which produce long reads, directly relevant to resolving structural variants and repetitive regions that short-read WGS data can miss. |
